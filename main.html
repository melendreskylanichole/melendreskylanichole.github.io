Introduction to Surveying

SURVEYING | FORM | CONTACTS |



Surveying is the process of determining the relative position of natural and manmade features on or under the earth’s surface, the presentation of this information either graphically in the form of plans or numerically in the form of tables, and the setting out of measurements on the earth’s surface. It usually involves measurement, calculations, the production of plans, and the determination of specific locations. The surveyor may be called on to determine heights and distances; to set out buildings, bridges and roadways; to determine areas and volumes and to draw plans at a predetermined scale.
